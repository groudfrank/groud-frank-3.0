Wallet

Wallet is a concept for a finance application that focuses heavily on data visualization for the end user. It should tie in together very nicely a wide variety of financial information that is relavant to the user and their financial goals. This was my first data heavy illustration and I wanted to chellenge myself with providing an abundance of data in a user friendly manner.

Horizon

This one was mean to be clean and simple. Blur is one of those things where it either looks really good or really awful. I wanted to apply a bit of blur to create the illusion of a horizon without The use of solid colors or lines. It's also an example of how 1D objects on a canvas can produce a 2.5D end result. Shapes, and how they work together, are powerful tools for design.

Bermeja

Information you need to display at a glance should depend on the product being served. This is a landing page + hotel indexing web app for a fictional tropical island-nation. The goal here is to invite the user to buy into the product with the use of a great first impression via visuals as well ass providing the means to easily get started buying into what is being sold by providing relavant information at a glance. The slogan, option to explore more, sign-up/sign-in information are all made clearly visible to the user though optimal placement of those elements.

Adopt a Paw

It can feel like the term 'minimalism' is a loaded catch-phase used as a fad these days but in design in can be an incredibly powerful tool. Adopt-a-paw is an illustration for pet adoption organization and the end goal is to provide imagary with only the most relevant information to a variety of users(those who may want to adopt an animal vs someone who wants to volunteer their services). It gets straight to the point.

Fiore

Fiore is a demonstration of the use of responsive design to provide the same amount of features and information accross two common sources and extremes of display sizes - desktop and mobile phones. Different display media provide different opportunities to present data in a palatable way to the end user via proper UI and UX implementation.

Charge State

This project takes a quadurple approach to presenting data. Firstly, it makes use of traffic light color symbolism and hierchy to present different batttery states. Traffic light colors are symbolically universal and even invoke certain emotions (Red > Stop > Bad || Yellow > Caution > So So || Green > Go > Good). Secondly, it used shapes for further defferentiation. The last two elemnents are iconography and drop shadow layers.

Merch Store

With the rise of platforms like YouTube, Twitch, etc merch stores have become a main means of income for orgs, creatives and content creators. As with all my designs, I wanted to demonstrate that less is more and that clever placement of elements and options are important for providing a good user experience for the end user - which encourages them to buy. Pricing, color options, merchandise info are all avialable at a glance.

Gradients

Colors and design are as inseperable from each other and as symbiotic to each other as faith is to religion. In fact, color without any other design elements like text, geopmatric shapes, icons or textures can constitue design. Solid colors are rarely ever present in nature. A sunset can have varying levels of blues, pinks and oranges. Gradients are a pillar of modern design and can significantly enhance the visual experience. These are some of my favorite gradual gradient pallet.
